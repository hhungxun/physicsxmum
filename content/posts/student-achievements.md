## Student Testimonials

### Chen Jiaxin (Batch 2021/09)

Learning physics has never been an easy task. Fortunately, I have met many great teachers and companions on the road of learning physics.

The lecturers in the Department of Physics are very responsible and friendly. Every question after class is answered in a timely and patient manner. In daily lectures, our lecturers immerse themselves in the beauty of physics or mathematics in delivering physics knowledge to us. The excitement and devotion make us feel deeply connected to physics.

In my sophomore year, I also joined the Physics Society and met many friends from the department. Dining together from time to time, we chat and discuss our future directions, have occasional meetings to plan physics-related project activities and put them into practice. The activities outside of studies are fun and fulfilling.

With the advancement of my studies, I realised that learning should not be limited to textbook knowledge. The independent study is just a catalyst for us to explore unknown areas, driven by our own initiative, to satisfy our curiosity.

The Laboratory class in my sophomore year was my first real contact with physical experiments after the epidemic. From the experiments, I found that there is always a big gap between theory and reality. The self-designed experiment based on Arduino is a field that we had never set foot in before — we had to proactively self-learn simple programming, circuit connection, and data analysis. What impressed me most was the **poster presentation** at the end of the semester, where we invited lecturers and students to observe and guide us. The professors behave as both our teachers and friends.

### Leong Jing Tian (Batch 2021/04)

Most of our lecturers are very friendly, especially Assoc. Prof. Lim Yen Kheng and Prof. Tomasz Paterek. Prof. Lim explains concepts in detail and clearly, providing step-by-step derivations and notes. Prof. Tomasz gives exercise questions on the spot and points out errors directly during class time.

There were also some memorable experiences during the lab sessions. I joined the lab with seniors during the first physical lab session after the pandemic, because we had only two students in our intake. Fortunately, I met kind teammates who led me to complete the experiments. I also joined a lab session with juniors in my second lab session — and this semester, we had our first experience with poster presentations. It was enjoyable and memorable.

I work in multimedia for the XMUM Physics Society. We conducted our first event, the new student orientation, for two days in September. My job scope is to take photos and edit videos during the event, and to manage the society's social media account. It was really fun — and, of course, exhausting — but it was good for bonding between seniors and juniors.

### Yang Ziou (Batch 2020/09)

Welcome to the Physics Department in Xiamen University Malaysia! I'm an undergraduate student from Physics Department in 2020. Because I am very interested in the theory of everything, I chose to major in physics.

My first and second years were in the form of online courses due to COVID-19. Although I could not communicate with my teachers and classmates face to face, through online communications I learned thermodynamics through Prof. Ong, the charm of electromagnetism from Prof. Tomasz, optics from Prof. Kelvin, and quantum mechanics from Prof. Lim. One of my most memorable learning experiences was doing research with Prof. Lim Yen Kheng on **Hayward spacetime**. Prof. Lim first gave me books to read to understand the conceptual basis of the theory of relativity. Then he patiently, step by step, guided me on how to write my paper, how to conduct research, and how to explore the physics of solving Einstein's equations in Hayward spacetime — including observing the trajectory of charged particles.

A few days before the physical courses of my junior year were about to begin, the entire Physics Department held a welcoming ceremony for the newest physics intake. Prof. Ong, Assistant Professor Lim Yen Kheng and Dr. Chung Fei Fang gave us a wonderful speech. During the two-day ceremony, students from the department played many interactive games together and I deeply felt the unity and enthusiasm of the department.

## Physics Lab Module

The Physics Lab is a guided design lab module to stimulate students' creativity in designing experiments and instruments for investigating physics phenomena. Students are divided into groups and work as a team to solve problems. Each group designs an experiment about mechanics or thermodynamics incorporating **Arduino** as the main measurement tool, with a small budget allocated to buy accessories. There is no examination — 100% of the course mark is from continuous assessments: weekly discussions, a final report, and a poster presentation.

Notable student projects include:
- Investigation of centripetal acceleration on a rotating disk
- Measuring resistance at various temperatures using Arduino
- Investigation of pendulum motion with an accelerometer

## Publication Highlight

The Centre for Postgraduate Studies and Research congratulates **Yang Ziou** and **Associate Professor Dr. Lim Yen Kheng** for their article published in *Physical Review D* (Q1, impact factor 5.407):

> **"Structure of test magnetic fields and charged particle motion in the Hayward spacetime"**

The study is about the nature of electromagnetic interactions in a non-singular spacetime first discovered by Hayward in 2006. The exterior of the spacetime approximates the well-known Schwarzschild solution, and it may be possible that the Hayward spacetime can mimic astrophysical black holes.

- [Journal article](https://journals.aps.org/prd/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevD.105.124045)
- [arXiv preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.12124v2)

## Research Internship Exchange Programme with University of Gdańsk

The Department of Physics of XMUM has signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the Faculty of Mathematics, Physics and Informatics at the **University of Gdańsk, Poland**, to offer a Research Internship Student Exchange Programme for students of both departments.

University of Gdańsk is world-famous for discoveries in quantum information and hosts the **International Centre for Theory of Quantum Technologies**. Prof. Tomasz Paterek collaborates with the Centre on quantum correlations, Bell inequalities, foundations of physics, and quantum optics.

The inaugural cohort comprised **Mr. Ng Khai Shuen** and **Mr. Ong Ming Khai**, who participated in the programme during the April 2023 semester.

**Ng Khai Shuen** worked on general relativity minimally coupled to massless scalar fields, exploring solutions in spacetimes where gravity sometimes repulses instead of attracts. He reflected: *"I had a very good opportunity to gain exposure — not just to different cultures and new people, but to see how different people approach physics outside of a local setting."*

**Ong Ming Khai** studied the relationship between solar activity and climate indicators, using Python to automate principal component analysis of historical sunspot numbers, climate data, and tree ring records. He said: *"The internship programme has allowed me to get a real feel of academic research, and I am very grateful to have the opportunity to conduct this research in a new environment."*

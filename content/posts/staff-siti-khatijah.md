## Biography

Dr. Siti Khatijah Md Saad graduated with a PhD from the Institute of Microengineering, Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia in 2018. Her research interests are mainly on the synthesis and characterization of nanomaterials as electrode materials for optoelectronic applications such as solar cells and supercapacitors. Currently, she is a Lab Officer for the Physics Department at Xiamen University Malaysia and handles the physics lab general elective courses.

## Research Interests

- Synthesis of porous-like metal oxide for organic hybrid perovskite solar cells
- Synthesis of (001) faceted pristine and doped metal oxide nanostructures for Dye-Sensitized Solar Cells and photocatalytic degradation of pollutants
- Synthesis of transition metal dichalcogenides for optoelectronic applications
- Synthesis of noble metal nanomaterials for current collectors in supercapacitors
- Activated carbon derived from palm oil residue for supercapacitor applications

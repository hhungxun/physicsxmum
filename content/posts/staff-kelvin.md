## Biography

Kelvin J. A. Ooi is an Associate Professor at Xiamen University Malaysia. He received his BEng (Hons.) and PhD degrees in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, in 2010 and 2014, respectively. He has authored over 50 international journal and conference publications with a citation count of over 500 and h-index of 14. He has also received and presented 8 invited talks in international conferences.

He is the recipient of several national and university research grants, and has secured over RM 300,000 in research funding from the Ministry of Higher Education Malaysia (FRGS) and the XMUM Research Fund as both Principal and Co-Investigator.

Prior to joining Xiamen University Malaysia, he served as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow (2013–2018) in the SUTD-MIT International Design Center and the Lee Kuan Yew Center for Innovative Cities at Singapore University of Technology and Design. He has worked on projects spanning integrated optics for optical computing, data transmission, and daylighting technologies for sustainable architecture.

His current research interests span interdisciplinary domains, primarily in the modelling of physical systems and its application to different domains of knowledge, including urban traffic and neural propagation.

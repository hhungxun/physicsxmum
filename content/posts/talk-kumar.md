## Abstract

**Title: A Bosonic Open System Approach to Superconducting Nanodevices**

Josephson junctions are the heart of superconducting devices that underpin emerging quantum technologies. Despite significant attention, a theoretically consistent description of Cooper pair dynamics is still an open question. We build a robust theoretical framework for studying the dynamics of arbitrary nanodevices and utilise it to propose a dissipationless geometry for pumping a supercurrent.

Cooper pairs in the terminals of Josephson junctions are treated as bosonic open quantum systems governed by a Markovian master equation. Although phenomenological at large, our model opens up new avenues to envision novel superconducting platforms for probing the theory of open quantum systems.

## About the Speaker

The speaker finished his PhD studies at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee in India, with a strong collaboration at the University of Gdańsk in Poland. He is currently a postdoctoral researcher in the **New Quantum Resources and Thermodynamics group** at the International Centre for Theory of Quantum Technologies (ICTQT) in Gdańsk. His current research is focused on spin chain dynamics and a bosonic open system model for superconducting nanodevices.

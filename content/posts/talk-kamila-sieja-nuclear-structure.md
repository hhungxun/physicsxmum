## Abstract

The **Configuration Interaction Shell Model (CI-SM)** is a powerful theoretical framework used to investigate the nuclear structure of atomic nuclei. In this talk, Dr. Sieja will briefly outline the fundamental principles of nuclear structure and the various approaches employed to solve the nuclear many-body problem. She will then delve into the CI-SM framework, highlighting its capabilities in describing nuclear structure phenomena. Additionally, she will discuss the limitations of the model and explore potential strategies to overcome these challenges.

## About the Speaker

Dr. Kamila Sieja (KS) finished her PhD in theoretical nuclear physics with a double degree from the Marie Curie-Skłodowska University in Poland and Université Bordeaux 1 in France. She was then a postdoctoral researcher at GSI Darmstadt and IPHC Strasbourg. She holds a permanent position at CNRS since 2012 and now heads the **theory group at IPHC Strasbourg**. Her research is focused on nuclear structure and reactions with applications to astrophysics.

My name is Thurston Foo, currently a fourth-year student, and I have recently completed my internship at the University of Gdańsk in Poland. My experience there has been nothing short of amazing; I made new friends, precious memories, and learnt many new things both academically and outside of it. I had already expected an incredible experience after hearing so many stories from seniors who had their internships there previously — but after coming back I can confidently say that it completely blew my expectations out of the water. Truly, it was an immensely fulfilling journey from beginning to end.

## The Research

I worked under the supervision of Dr. Thomas Złosnik (Prof. Tom) in a group with my fellow classmate Yun Ten, exploring and developing cosmological consequences of an extension to General Relativity — one which was conceived by Prof. Tom and his colleague. They found a set of models of gravitation which includes both Einstein's theory of General Relativity and some other model, which we call **Model B**. This Model B is distinctive in that its gravitational field has no degrees of freedom and has more symmetry than General Relativity. We studied Model B in FRW symmetry, deriving its equations of motion and studying its implications.

## Working at the PhD Incubator

Work every day took place in the Institute of Theoretical Physics and Astrophysics, in the **'PhD Incubator'**. What I would do at the office may not sound exciting — it typically involves reading up on materials provided or suggested by Prof. Tom, performing calculations, and deriving equations — but I looked forward to it every day. Up to twice a week we would meet with Prof. Tom to discuss research, its content and progress, and have our usual chatter about anything in particular.

It was always a joy to talk with Prof. Tom, be it about physics or day-to-day life. He is a fantastic mentor endowed with a wealth of knowledge which he generously shares with us. I could not be more grateful for his guidance throughout my internship. Aside from a great supervisor, the faculty members and other students are also all so amicable and down to earth — I have only met wonderful people in the physics faculty there.

## Spring in Poland

My stay in Poland had been a treat. I arrived in Gdańsk just after winter had waned and had the opportunity to relish springtime there — watching the flowers bloom had been a feast for my eyes. Never had I witnessed barren trees slowly turn thick and green, and empty plains suddenly burst and pop brightly with budding flowers. The weather was great (really cold, but I enjoyed it) and I took delight in walking around the city and at parks scattered all around.

Besides the city of Gdańsk, I also explored the surrounding region — the tri-city area of Gdańsk, Gdynia, and Sopot offers stunning coastal scenery and a wealth of culture and history that made every weekend an adventure.

## A Word to Future Students

If you are considering an international research internship, I cannot recommend it highly enough. The combination of rigorous academic work, cultural immersion, and personal growth that comes from living and researching abroad is truly unparalleled. The Physics Department's partnership with the University of Gdańsk is a remarkable opportunity — seize it if you can.

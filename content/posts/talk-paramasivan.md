## Abstract

**Title: Atomic Nuclei as a Testing Ground for Quantum Algorithms and a Quantum Information Resource**

This talk will begin with a summary of recent results from Prof. Arumugam's group, with interconnected interests ranging from quantum information and computing in many-body systems to astrophysics and astronomy. The focus then shifts to recent developments in nuclear physics related to quantum algorithms and quantum information.

While quantum many-body problems are inherent to several physical systems, the application of quantum information concepts to nuclear physics might require more attention — and can potentially open several new applications. Recent efforts in this regard will be outlined, with results for Coulomb scattering obtained by solving explicitly the time-dependent Schrödinger equation.

## About the Speaker

**Prof. Paramasivan Arumugam** obtained his doctoral degree in 2003 from MS University in Tirunelveli for his work on theoretical nuclear physics. He conducted post-doctoral research at the Institute of Physics Bhubaneswar, RIKEN Japan, and IST Lisbon, Portugal. He joined IIT Roorkee in 2008 and is presently a **Professor in the Department of Physics and the Centre for Photonics and Quantum Communication**. As a former Dean of International Relations and the Organising Chairman for GATE 2025, he has also been active in administrative roles.

Hello everyone, I am a second-year physics student at Xiamen University Malaysia Campus. My name is Yong Jing Tian, and I am from Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. Xiamen University Malaysia is a campus with beautiful scenery, and what brought me to this university was a subject that seems to encompass everything, yet is also mysterious — physics. As one of the few higher education institutions in Malaysia that offers a pure theoretical physics programme, XMUM naturally became my first choice.

## Academic Life in the Physics Department

The physics department at Xiamen University Malaysia Campus provides quality education for students who love physics, paving the way for those who dream of pursuing it. In the past two academic years, the department has provided a series of professional courses in mathematics and physics. In these two years, I have learned classical mechanics, electromagnetism, thermodynamics, and quantum mechanics. Mathematics is the queen of physics, and the courses have expanded my knowledge of calculus, linear algebra, probability theory, ordinary differential equations, partial differential equations, and complex analysis.

The courses also cover various software and programming languages commonly used in physical and mathematical research, including Python, Matlab, Wolfram Mathematica, and Comsol Multiphysics, and we learned how to use LaTeX to write professional academic papers meeting academic standards.

## Beyond the Classroom

Here, besides the professional courses, we can also choose from various elective courses. During one short semester, I took a course called **"Invisible Science"**, where I learned about optics and complex analysis — and how to achieve electromagnetic invisibility using the geometric structure of metamaterials.

XMUM also provides various platforms for self-directed learning, such as academic seminars. Our Physics Student Society organised a five-week lecture series covering quantum mechanics and elements of group theory. Beyond formal events, I also met many senior students who are passionate about physics — they generously shared their guidance and pointed me towards research directions.

## A Journey to NUS

At the end of July 2023, I participated in a visit to the Department of Physics at the **National University of Singapore (NUS)**. This trip opened up a new world for me. Reading ten thousand books is not as valuable as travelling ten thousand miles — my experiences at XMUM, past, present, and future, illuminate my shining dreams. Here, I encountered more of the world, and met many, many people walking the same path of pursuing physics. This swept away a loneliness I had felt — the loneliness of living among people who couldn't understand why I chose to study physics.

## Finding a Sense of Belonging

Here, I have found a sense of belonging.

Although the Physics Department at XMUM was established relatively recently, I see in it the academic atmosphere I had imagined for research — a resonance of people united in the common goal of advancing physics. I enjoy my journey studying at XMUM, and this institution has laid the foundation for the dreams I chase. I hope future students can have an unforgettable and wonderful university experience here too.

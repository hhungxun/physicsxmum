## Abstract

**Title: Plasmonic Metafiber Devices**

Metasurfaces are 2D artificial materials made of subwavelength elementary cells that give rise to unexpected wave properties that do not exist naturally. By rationally configuring the shape, size, position, and orientation of unit cells with high spatial resolution, one can manipulate the phase, amplitude and polarisation of light and thus engineer its wavefront at will.

Optical fibre tips that combine fibre optics and planar technologies are emerging as one of the most promising light-coupling platforms for nanophotonic devices. Integrating plasmonic metasurfaces on optical fibre tips to form **metafibers** expands the functionalities of an ordinary optical fibre to nanoscale manipulation of light, yielding a variety of advanced applications such as planar waveshaping, super-resolution imaging, and ultracompact sensing.

This talk will first give a brief review on metafiber devices explored in the past five years and then focus on the **metafiber electro-optic modulator** as a detailed example.

## About the Speaker

**Jiyong Wang** is a professor at Hangzhou Dianzi University, China. He received his double PhD degrees in Physical and Theoretical Chemistry from Eberhard Karls University of Tübingen, Germany, and in Nanoscience and Technology from the University of Technology of Troyes, France in 2017. He then joined the near-field optics group at CNRS-University of Bourgogne as a postdoctoral researcher for one year. From 2018 to 2022, he worked as a research assistant professor at Westlake University, China. Since then, he has been a full-time professor at Hangzhou Dianzi University. His research interests include nanophotonics, optical engineering, and their cross-disciplinary applications in ocean science.

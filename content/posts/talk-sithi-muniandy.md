## Abstract

**Title: Brownian Motion and Its Generalisation for Modelling Classical and Quantum Dynamics**

Brownian motion or classical random walk has played a pivotal role in our understanding of the atomistic view of physical phenomena, fluctuations and transport phenomena. Brownian motion $B(t)$ is a Gaussian fractal stochastic process with independent increments that finds many applications in the modelling of Markovian dynamics.

Using fractional calculus and other mathematical generalisations, one can extend Brownian motion to model anomalous transport, long-memory processes, and non-Markovian dynamics. This talk will explore these generalisations and their applications to both classical systems — including anomalous diffusion in biological systems — and quantum systems, where noise plays a fundamental role in decoherence dynamics of quantum walks and quantum entanglement.

## About the Speaker

**Prof. Sithi V Muniandy** received his DPhil in Mathematics from the University of Oxford (1998) for his work on wavelet multiscale theory of turbulence. His research covers a broad field of statistical physics, including anomalous transport phenomena, fluctuations in classical and quantum systems, decoherence dynamics of noisy quantum walk, and quantum entanglement in noisy environments. He also collaborates with biologists to explore applications of theoretical physics models to systems biology.

Prof. Sithi served as Deputy Dean of Research at the Faculty of Science, Dean of the Frontier Science Research Cluster, and Senate member of **Universiti Malaya**. He currently heads the **Centre for Theoretical and Computational Physics** at Universiti Malaya.

He is also an advocate of the artscience approach in STEM education — together with his students, he produced physics theatre (*ATOMYSTIC – Life and Works of Boltzmann*, 2016) and physics puppet shows (*Quantum Debate in Wayang Kulit*, 2018; *Quantum Revolutions in Wayang Kulit*, 2025).

**Internship place:** Institute of Theoretical Physics and Astrophysics, University of Gdańsk, Poland
**Internship period:** September to December 2023

My name is Yu Xuan and at the time of writing this I am currently a year-3 student finishing his internship and Undergraduate Research II at the University of Gdańsk in Poland. It has been a few months since I arrived in Gdańsk and I got to say that my life has been fantastic here.

## First Snow

The first thing I would like to share is that as a 21-year-old born and raised in Malaysia, I had the chance to witness and touch snow for the very first time in my life, which is truly incredible. The texture of snow is exactly the same as the shaved ice in cendol dessert in Malaysia, where our only three seasons are hot, hotter, and even hotter.

## The Research

The title of my research with Dr. Thomas Zlosnik is **"Action Integral and Newton-Cartan Theory"**.

Action integral is an integral used in physics like a dictionary that stores the physics concepts of a system — from which one can extract equations of motion, conservation laws, and more — provided that we know the Lagrangian of the system.

Newton-Cartan theory allows expressing Newtonian gravity equations with spacetime symmetries similar to Einstein's General Relativity. The question is: can we write out an action in a way such that the equations of Newton-Cartan theory can be extracted from it? So far, such an integral has not been discovered in our research. However, we have developed all the essential tools and mathematics needed to unveil its form in the future.

## Community and Life at the Institute

Another thing I enjoyed during my stay was the community I found here. In the department, there are Master's and PhD defences from time to time — I participated in one Master's defence and attended several physics talks. These events gave me a clearer image of what to expect in graduate school in the coming years.

Sometimes I would go out for dinner gatherings with my team members and try dishes I have never seen before — and they taste incredible. I also experienced my first ever soccer match in an arena, watching the players live at Polsat Arena Gdańsk. It was remarkable to be surrounded by cheering fans.

## Friendships and Adventures

Last but not least, the most precious memory that will always have a special place in my heart is the time with all the amazing friends I made here. All of us come from different parts of the world and somehow we ended up meeting together in this one place.

We had weekly Friday night gatherings, went to escape rooms, speedran the entire Harry Potter movie series in a month, wrestled in the snow and had snowball fights. We went from Gdańsk in the north all the way to Zakopane in the south, hiking, snowboarding and skiing, and had a campfire with sausages and cheese.

As Winnie the Pooh once said: *"We didn't realize we were making memories; we just knew we were having fun."*

## Reflections

This semester has been the greatest and the happiest one for me in my entire university life as a physics student. I am very grateful to everyone who made it possible for me to come here for my internship and Undergraduate Research II.

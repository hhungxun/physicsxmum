*Published on June 16, 2023*

From 31 May to 2 June 2023, the Department of Physics and the China-ASEAN College of Marine Sciences at Xiamen University Malaysia collaborated to support the **First Interdisciplinary Science Camp** of Sabah Tshung Tsin Secondary School.

## Programme Highlights

Dr. Teoh Hong Wooi from the China-ASEAN College of Marine Sciences gave a talk on the importance of Marine Science. His team, consisting of Nina Ho Ann Jin, Tay Yii Jing, and Teh Seok Qi, guided the students in printing a fish using a traditional Japanese method known as **Gyotaku**.

Representing the Department of Physics were Dr. Chung Fei Fang and two students, Liew Dek Hao and Zhang Weibao. In the talk **"Games of Fractals"**, Dr. Chung used computer games to engage the audience in the world of physics. During the hands-on activity, Liew Dek Hao and Zhang Weibao demonstrated how a complex structure emerges by repeating a simple rule on paper cutting.

## Reflections from Participants

**Tay Yii Jing:** "It was an enjoyable and great experience to join the CSI STEAM Camp, especially since it is my first time joining a Science Camp. I was happy to share some of my experiences as a Marine Biotechnology student, and had a fun time assisting the students in the gyotaku activities. It was a pleasure to meet everyone — thank you for giving me this opportunity!"

**Teh Seok Qi:** "It was a very special experience to join the CSI STEAM science camp as a teaching assistant. Sharing my university experiences as a Marine Biotechnology person with Tshung Tshin High School students reminded me of all the fun memories throughout these years at XMUM. I was also amazed by the art and crafts skills students showed during the gyotaku sessions. A big appreciation to everyone who made this camp successful!"

**Liew Dek Hao:** "The CSI Steam camp at Sabah Tshung Tsin Secondary School was an amazing experience, which gave me an opportunity to share my knowledge with enthusiastic students. The school provided a lively setting that encouraged learning and creativity. Seeing the students' excitement during the hands-on project and workshop made me realize the importance of nurturing their curiosity and passion for STEAM subjects. This camp reminded me of education's impact on young minds and reinforced my dedication to empowering students. I would like to express my heartfelt gratitude to the Department of Physics at XMUM for giving me this incredible opportunity."

**Zhang Weibao:** "This is my first time visiting Sabah. When the plane landed, I was already captivated by the unique and beautiful natural landscape. The students participated in the activities and answered questions actively, and their eyes were filled with a thirst for knowledge and curiosity about science. The teachers and students from CSI organized the activities with careful planning, making the whole event smooth and successful. It was an unforgettable experience to be a teaching assistant and I really cherish the friends I made there. Sincerely hope to see them again!"
